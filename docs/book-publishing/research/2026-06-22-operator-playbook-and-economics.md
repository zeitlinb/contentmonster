# Operator playbook + economics for Brad's authority-book series

## Headline finding (challenges the "revenue product" call)

**Every credible operator, the unit economics, and both adversarial critics converge: run the
~5-book series as an AUTHORITY/FUNNEL play, not a revenue play.** Self-pub nonfiction averages
**<250 lifetime copies; ~90% sell <100.** At $4.99 / ~$3.43 net that's a few hundred dollars per
book — a rounding error against **one** backend client into GTM Machines / openclaw / Hermes
(Shreeve's model converts $4.99 buyers into $3K–$30K engagements). Brad uniquely has the funnel
prerequisites most authors lack: **existing authority, real high-ticket offers, and capital.**

**Important nuance — "funnel-first," not "funnel-only," and the build is nearly identical either way.**
Whichever KPI we pick, the execution is the same: ~25k words, $4.99 ebook / $13.99 paperback, wide,
real name, human-written, strong covers, no related-party reviews. The decision changes mainly **(a)
what we optimize and measure** (royalties vs qualified leads) and **(b) whether the back-of-book funnel
architecture is a mandatory launch precondition.** So this is a low-cost decision to make — but it's
**Brad's**, and the evidence now points away from his earlier "revenue product" pick.

## Operators studied

- **Tommi Pedruzzi (Brad's reference) — study the MECHANICS, reject the MODEL.** His real business is a **$99/mo course/community**, not book royalties; the "$3M" is cumulative-since-2019, skewed to course income (per the PirateWires investigation). His edge is **mining 1–3★ reviews of bestsellers → publishing "improved" clones under SEO pseudonyms** — anonymous AI-volume. **Lift:** niche validation via Amazon Suggest, ChatGPT outline, Claude long-form, Grammarly/Hemingway polish, Canva/Ideogram cover, "shorter wins" (he cut books in half, says sales doubled), "the cover gets you paid." **Reject (account-ending for a real-name brand):** pseudonyms, undisclosed AI, competitor-cloning, review-mining-to-republish.
- **The credible authority-book cluster** (the model to copy): **Mike Capuzzi** ("shooks"/100-Page Book — most on-target), **Dan Kennedy** ("Book The Business" — funnel-book doctrine, "big money without selling a single copy"), **Honoree Corder** (productized repeatable system), **Rob Fitzpatrick** ("Write Useful Books" — the "recommendable" quality bar), **Tucker Max / Zach Obront (Scribe)** (CEO authority books, talk-it-out drafting), **Allan Dib** (1-Page Marketing Plan, 1M+ copies as a lead magnet), plus **Marcus Sheridan, Dorie Clark, Joe Pulizzi, Nick Pavlidis**.
- **Recurring playbook:** one named framework · short · real expertise (dictate/record) · human-written prose · physical paperback as credential · back-of-book CTA into a high-ticket offer · book is the *top* of the funnel, not the product.

## Domain demand — which title first
- **AI Chief of Staff → FIRST.** Genuine **white space** — big tool/market buzz, but **no authority book owns the term/buyer**. Native CTA into openclaw/Hermes; a chance to **define the category**. (Disambiguate hard from "Chief AI Officer".)
- **GEO / AEO ("get found by ChatGPT/LLMs") → SECOND.** Highest demand but **saturated** with thin clones shipping weekly. Win only with a **named framework + narrow buyer + year stamp**; keep third-party model names out of the dominant subtitle slot; plan annual editions (decays fast).
- **GTM Engineering → THIRD.** Freshest open field (exploding role/salary data), but **narrower B2B buyer**; tie to GTM Machines.
- Book #4 "AI-Native Operator" (umbrella): **highest duplicate-content risk** — re-scope with a distinct framework or defer until the first 3 prove out.

## Economics (web-verified, 2026)
- Ebook 70% band = **$2.99–$9.99**; outside = 35%. Delivery fee **$0.15/MB** (70% plan only) → keep the file <1MB. **$4.99 → ~$3.43/sale.** $0.99 → 35% → ~$0.35 (launch only).
- Paperback royalty tiered (since Jun 2025): **60% at $9.99+**, 50% below, minus print cost. B&W 6×9 ~110pp print ≈ $2.40–$2.80 → **$13.99 → ~$5.59/sale.**
- KU/KENP ~**$0.0045/page**; full ~110pp read ≈ $0.60–$0.75. KDP Select = 90-day **auto-renewing** ebook exclusivity → blocks the free lead-magnet use → **go wide.**
- Length consensus: short authority/lead-magnet = **~15–25k words / ~60–110pp**; floor ~18k to clear the credibility/thin-content bar. Target **~22–28k words, 8–12 chapters, one framework.**
- Payments: **EFT = no threshold**, ~60 days after month-end; check/wire hold until $100/€100 per marketplace.

## The genuine forks (Brad decides)
1. **Funnel vs revenue KPI** — rec: **funnel-first** (evidence above).
2. **Production mode per book** — rec: **Mode A (human-written, AI-assisted)** → honest "unchecked" disclosure + real authority + enforceable copyright.
3. **Launch traction w/o review violations** — rec: drive **buys**, not reviews, from Brad's audiences.
4. **Personal vs entity tax** — rec: personal name (speed + authority); SMLLC uses owner's TIN. [VERIFY w/ accountant.]
5. **Own ISBN vs free** — rec: free KDP ASIN/ISBN (own ISBN only for a named imprint via IngramSpark later).
6. **Ebook-only vs +paperback/+hardcover** — rec: ebook + paperback; hardcover flagship/post-launch.
7. **KU vs wide** — rec: **wide.**
8. **First title** — rec: **AI Chief of Staff.**
9. **Keep/defer book #4** — rec: re-scope or defer.

## Top risks (account-critical first)
1. ⛔ **Undisclosed AI-generated text** — AI-first-drafted = "AI-generated" even after edits; mis-declaring = takedown/suspension across all 5 books. → Mode A or check the box; keep an AI-usage log.
2. ⛔ **Review manipulation** — no reviews from anyone personally/financially connected (Amazon address-book-matches). → buys not reviews; bar beta readers.
3. **Single-account blast radius** — all 5 + Author Central + Brad's name on one account; off-Amazon free PDFs keep the funnel alive if a listing is flagged.
4. **AI-slop brand damage** — Brad's name IS the asset; the blocking "recommendable" beta gate + real recorded expertise are load-bearing.
5. **Duplicate content across 5 same-author books** — distinct frameworks/examples/prose + cross-book similarity gate; #4 is the worst offender.
6. **Trademark — Brad's own names** ("Hermes" crowded) — USPTO clearance before publishing.
7. **Thin copyright on AI-drafted prose** — purely AI text isn't registrable → default Mode A.
8. **Funnel leak** — ~70% of free readers never act without strong capture → funnel-live is a publish precondition.
9. **Operator numbers are unverified marketing** — treat mechanics as credible, magnitudes as aspirational; build no projections on them.
