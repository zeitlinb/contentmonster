## Confirmations (the canonical loop everyone repeats)
Research (Amazon keyword + bestsellers) → outline mapping each chapter to ONE reader problem →
draft (AI-assisted) → strong cover (Canva) → optimize title/keywords/categories → publish (KDP, ~10 min) →
promote via email list. Per @grok's own step list and multiple guides. Matches our runbook 1:1.
- **$4.99 ebook = the repeated sweet spot**; nonfiction 2026 ranges: ebook $4.99–$14.99, print $14.99–$24.99, audiobook $14.95–$24.95.
- **Go WIDE for nonfiction** (Apple/Kobo/B&N) — confirmed.
- **Email list >> social**: "500–1,000 engaged subscribers outperform 50,000 cold followers on launch day."
- "One reader, one problem" focus = the authority-book best practice. "Finished beats perfect."

## Refinements to fold into the runbook (factual KDP mechanics)
1. **Category/keyword ranking mechanics:** Amazon weights ~**60% category relevance / 40% keyword match** for search rank. The MOST SPECIFIC category (e.g. "Business > Small Business > Entrepreneurship") drives **~3.4× more qualified traffic** than a broad one; use the 3 slots across **different hierarchies**. Long-tail keywords convert far better ("how to start a business" ≈ **12× "business"**). Give keywords **30–60 days** before changing. → enrich runbook 6.4.
2. **Algorithm now favors LONG-TERM impact over launch bursts** ("short sales bursts are less effective than they once were"; rotate categories when sales plateau). This **tempers the $0.99 launch-spike emphasis** — sustained relevance > a one-week spike. Relevant to the hybrid KPI and the GEO annual-refresh plan.

## ⚠ Contradiction to resolve (compliance)
Mainstream advice here says **"send ARCs to your email list + beta readers, then a review request"** —
which **directly collides with our account-ending review gate** (no reviews from anyone personally
connected; Amazon address-book-matches). Reconciliation: a **back-matter review request to general
(arms-length) buyers is fine**; **soliciting your own list/beta readers to REVIEW is the risky part.**
Drive your list to BUY; let reviews come arms-length / via Request-a-Review. (The popular advice is exactly the behavior our compliance research flags.)
