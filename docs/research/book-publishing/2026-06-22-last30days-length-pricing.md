# last30days — length & price norms (2026-06-22)

## What this changes (two genuine refinements, both tied to the HYBRID KPI)

1. **Price has UPSIDE on the revenue side — and Brad has the brand to use it.** Business/prescriptive
   nonfiction commonly commands **$6.99–$9.99** because readers pay for *results*, not pages (per
   theauthorcentral, hmdpublishing, analysis of 10k+ books). $4.99 and $9.99 are **both in the 70%
   band**, so royalty tier doesn't decide it — *brand strength* does, and Brad's companies supply that.
   → Revised posture: **$4.99 to launch / build audience → step to $6.99–$9.99 as the series earns
   authority** (esp. later titles; GEO already flagged for $6.99). This is the hybrid's revenue side
   having actual teeth — not "same as funnel."  ($4.99 → ~$3.49/sale; $9.99 → ~$6.84/sale.)
2. **Length has a hybrid band.** Mainstream "ideal nonfiction" = **40–80k words** (business ~40–50k);
   short-format (Kindle Singles) = **5–30k**. Our authority spec (~22–28k) is right for the funnel/
   authority play (Capuzzi "100-page book", "shorter wins"). **If revenue weight rises in the hybrid,
   nudge toward ~30–40k** for standalone-product legitimacy — still "short" vs the 40–80k standard,
   but meatier than a pure lead magnet. (233 words/page nonfiction → 25k ≈ 107pp; 35k ≈ 150pp.)

## Pricing philosophy worth stealing (backend)
- **Value/transformation pricing, not length pricing** — per @kongtaner: "$297 beats $197… price the
  transformation." That's the **backend** info-product/offer model ($197–$297), where the real money
  is; the KDP book stays cheap as the front-end. Directly reinforces the funnel/hybrid architecture.
- **Funnel doctrine, third independent confirmation** — Fabienne Finlay (419k views): "STOP selling on
  KDP… Tony Robbins / Grant Cardone *give books away free* and make money on the backend." Same thesis
  the operator research reached; now triple-confirmed across runs.
